📰 Latest news (June 2, 2026)
We have begun a research period focusing on Zeitwerk, our new free and open-source timestamping service, thanks to support from the Prototype Fund.
We are also deploying evidx.de to pilot media partners. Built in partnership with Dot•Studio, bootstrapped by the Babelsberg Media Innovation Center.

The Toolkit is an umbrella for several research strands: a (hopefully) coherent set of questions about how digital evidence gets made, broken, and made trustworthy again, with tools & demos built to answer those questions in practice.

Each project is independent but they share a baseplate of principles and design choices that make them snap together. From that shared problem, each tool is grounded in the practice of digital investigations, journalism and human rights documentation.

Shared baseplate principles and ideas

  • 🔐 Cryptographic hashing (SHA-256, Merkle trees)
  • 📋 Append-only, tamper-evident audit trails
  • 🔗 Open, interoperable formats (WACZ, RFC 3161, C2PA)
  • 🇪🇺 EU-grounded infrastructure
  • ⚖️ Designed for legal admissibility from day one
